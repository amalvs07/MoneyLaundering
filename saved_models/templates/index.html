F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

Any individual or organization that handles large amounts of cash or engages in international financial transactions is at risk of money laundering.

Money laundering involves disguising the proceeds of illegal activity as legitimate funds, whereas tax evasion involves failing to pay taxes that are owed on legitimate income.

Money laundering can have a negative impact on the economy by distorting financial markets, facilitating corruption, and enabling criminal activities.

Banks play a critical role in combating money laundering by implementing AML policies and procedures, monitoring transactions for suspicious activity, and reporting suspicious transactions to authorities.

Businesses can protect themselves from money laundering by implementing anti-money laundering (AML) policies and procedures, conducting due diligence on customers and business partners, and monitoring transactions for suspicious activity.

Legal money is income earned through legitimate means, whereas illegal money is income earned through criminal activity.
